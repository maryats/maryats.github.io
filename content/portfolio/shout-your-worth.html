Shout Your Worth is a game-like toolkit to help you communicate your value. It teaches the value statement format for talking about their accomplishments — which is a proven technique taught in salary negotiation workshops put on by the AAUW.

Shout Your Worth was created for the Interaction Design Senior Capstone team project at Northeastern University.

Players craft value statements using Accomplishment, Skill, and Result cards, then share real-world experiences that illustrate those value statements. Collaborative gameplay creates a supportive, encouraging environment for players to practice this self-advocacy skill.

A "Wicked Problem"

Research

We surveyed working professionals and fellow college students to learn about their experiences negotiating for their salary, and the gender wage gap. Something that really stood out to us was the polarized attitude toward negotiation — it seemed that there was a confidence gap that wasn't being addressed by existing solutions.

Those who didn't plan to negotiate said things like:

“I don’t feel like I’m qualified or confident enough.”
“…I feel like I won’t have any leverage to negotiate.”

While those who did were confident about doing so:

“I know my skills are valuable and it’s always worth asking... I don’t know why you wouldn’t negotiate.”

After researching existing solutions for teaching salary negotiation, we found a gap that we could fill. Our solution would meet women where they are and encourage them to practice negotiation skills in a low pressure environment – what better way to do that than with a game?

Values-based Game Design

Our research inspired us to make a solution that would make people feel more confident about advocating for themselves, to help bridge the gap we saw in survey responses.

How can we create a chain reaction of education and empowerment by encouraging women to confidently discuss salary, negotiation and resources in a comfortable environment?

Following the principles of value-driven design we learned from Values at Play, we identified the key values and concepts we wanted our game to embody.

Iteration and Playtesting

We used basic paper prototypes to iterate quickly and get our ideas in front of players as soon as possible.

We used the feedback from each playtest to revise the rules — from mechanics to phrasing and format.

Visual Design

To appeal to our target audience of women at crossroads in their careers, we created a clean, friendly, celebratory, visual language that uses lovingly-crafted watercolor textures and patterns for a hand-crafted touch.

Website Development

I helped design and build a marketing website to give Shout Your Worth an online presence.
