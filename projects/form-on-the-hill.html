There the form stood, motionless as the hill beneath.

This print was inspired by passages in Thomas Hardy’s Return of the Native. I was inspired by the description of Eustacia Vye standing silhouetted on the barrow, just before the other villagers light bonfires across the heath.

This project was my first foray into linoleum printmaking, and enabled me to play around with different color combinations as a way of conveying different atmospheres.

January 2014
3-color linocut print on paper, edition of 23.
18 x 24″

Ink on the linoleum block, in cutting stage 2 of 3.

Different color palettes on the drying rack.

An impromptu triptych of color combinations.
